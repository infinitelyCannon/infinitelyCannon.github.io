Catformer

Catformer is an endless 2D platformer. The player is a cat facing the challenge of jumping up platforms and dodging hazards. The aim of the experience is to offer a challenge that is simple to execute (with its one, tap to jump mechanic) and engaging to react to. Along with the draw of offering multiple options for cat customization and aesthetically appealing art and music.

Roles:

• Random placement of platforms

There are three types of platforms the player jumps on depending on the stage they’re in and the task was to spawn them in a way that dissuades players from relying on one pattern to guarantee a safe upward path.

• Repeatedly scrolling parallax effect with background sprites

Worked with the environment artist to reach the desired look of two background layers scrolling at different speeds as the player moves. The backgrounds for each stage are to loop for a set number of times, then transition to the next stage before looping again up until the final endless space stage.

• Implemented primary tap to jump mechanic

The most significant lessons I learned from this project were the difficulties of getting assets and gameplay to look and act the same way on multiple mobile resolutions. Once the backgrounds were set to scale appropriately to match the screen, further adjustments were needed to keep the length in each stage and transitions between them consistent.

4715 Jetsam

4715 Jetsam is a third person platforming shooter. The player acts as a robot pirate that retrieves a lost treasure from a hostile alien planet. The target experience is an engaging pull through a science fiction setting through exploration, storytelling, and combat.

Roles:

• Implementing player movement and combat mechanics

The ability to move and look around with common third person control scheme, and fire from third and first-person perspectives.

• Implementing enemy A.I. system

Created a finite state machine to drive enemy behavior of keeping their distance from the player while firing, and patrolling.

• Creating a text-based menu system

Made text component objects that traverses a pre-determined menu layout based on player input.

A significant takeaway from this project is the importance of having concise documentation describing the goal for fulfilling a task or feature. Along with how much the Unity engine does and does not supply for you when branching from a common set of player mechanics.
